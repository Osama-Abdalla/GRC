ISO 27001 Business Case — CloudPay FinTech LLC
(Built from stakeholder pressure mapping)

1. The Pressure Driving This Program
Critical pressure:
  • CEO — $1M deal lost on ISO question. Wants cert in 9 months.
  • NY DFS — Licensing obligation requires documented security governance.
  • Enterprise customer (Bank X) — Security questionnaire scoring blocks contract signing.

High pressure:
  • CTO — Wants ISMS that doesn't slow engineering velocity.
  • CISO — Needs governance, ownership, and evidence framework.
  • Compliance Officer — Cannot produce evidence on demand. Wants defensible records.

Medium pressure:
  • SOC Provider — Outsourced. Needs co-operation on monitoring evidence.
  • HR Lead — Runs annual awareness training; wants better completion tracking.
  • Senior Engineer — Cares about secrets management hygiene and dev pipeline.

Low pressure:
  • Marketing — Wants the ISO logo on the website for trust.
  • Office Manager — Asking whether they need badge readers on meeting rooms.

2. Implementation Verdict
The implementation has clear top-down demand from leadership and external partners. The program owner is the CISO with the CEO as executive sponsor. The first 90 days will focus on context, scope, and risk methodology — not on Annex A controls.

3. Expected Implementation Outputs
ISMS scope statement, context register, interested-party register, information security policy, ISMS RACI, risk methodology, risk register, risk treatment plan, Statement of Applicability, control implementation tracker, evidence register, internal audit plan, management review pack, certification readiness report.
